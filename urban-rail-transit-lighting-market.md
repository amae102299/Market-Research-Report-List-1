<p><p>The urban rail transit lighting market is comprised of three main types: fluorescent, halogen, and LED. Fluorescent lighting uses gas discharge to produce light, while halogen lighting uses a tungsten filament surrounded by a mixture of halogen gases. On the other hand, LED lighting utilizes a semiconductor diode that emits light when an electric current passes through it. Each type has its advantages and disadvantages, such as energy efficiency, cost, and lifespan, which influence their adoption in urban rail transit systems.</p></p>
